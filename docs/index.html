How to Play

Players alternate turns, moving their pawns along the lines into vacant intersections on the board in an effort to capture enemy pawns. Capturing pawns is done via the custodian method, whereby a player positions two of their pawns on opposite sides of an opponent's pawn. Captures can only be performed during the turn of the player making the capture. Captured pieces are removed from the board at the end of a playerâ€™s turn. The game is won when your opponent is reduced to a single pawn, or is no longer able to move any of their pawns.
